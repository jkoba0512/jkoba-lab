        # About jkoba Lab

        **jkoba Lab** is a research group led by Associate Professor Jun Kobayashi
        in the **Department of Intelligent and Control Systems**, Graduate School of
        Computer Science and Systems Engineering, Kyushu Institute of Technology.

        Our research is rooted in **biologically-inspired robotics**: we draw on
        principles from neural computation and human movement to design robots
        — and the controllers that drive them — for the physical world.

  - block: markdown

      title: '🎯 Mission'
      text: |-
        We are interested in closing the gap between mechanical robot motion and
        the smooth, flexible behavior seen in living systems. By combining ideas
        from neuroscience — liquid neural networks, active inference, the
        equilibrium-point hypothesis — with biomechanically faithful musculoskeletal
        models, we look for control laws that remain robust in unfamiliar environments.

        - **Active Inference / Free Energy Principle** — goal-directed control of
          robot arms grounded in the free-energy principle
        - **Musculoskeletal Modeling** — biomechanically faithful arm models for
          studying reaching and motor control
