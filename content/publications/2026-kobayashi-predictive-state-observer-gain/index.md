abstract: "This work examines how forward-model-based predictive state observers should calibrate sensory prediction-error correction during muscle-driven reaching. Using a 34-muscle MyoSuite arm, intermediate correction gains are best with no sensory delay (K = 0.25–0.50), whereas observation-heavy correction wins under an 18-step delay (K = 1.0). Forward-model-only approaches underperform by 1.9–6.1 cm, while adaptive gain strategies partially compensate under delay by 1.9–2.5 cm. Feature-conditioned adapters nearly match per-cell diagnostics in 5 of 6 test cells but remain 1.4–1.8 cm below the oracle under delay conditions."
