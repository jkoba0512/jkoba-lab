abstract: "This study examines how reliability-weighted integration operates in musculoskeletal models, testing its application under conditions requiring adaptation or learning. Using a MyoSuite arm to estimate target positions, vision, proprioception, forward prediction, and task priors are integrated through precision weighting. A trusted but wrong prior biased the target estimate more as visual reliability decreased, and a false visual cue's influence varied with noise level. Precision weighting suffices under calibrated conditions, but adaptive statistics and learned source weighting become necessary when those assumptions are violated or miscalibrated."
