abstract: "Holding a redundant limb at a commanded posture against gravity is a basic problem in musculoskeletal motor control. Using the MyoSuite myoArm under a frozen-criterion, hash-provenanced protocol, this study evaluates fixed, non-learning controllers: an equilibrium-point/referent reflex, a reflex with co-contraction, an endpoint Cartesian impedance controller, gravity-compensation feedforward, and exact inverse-statics gravity balancing. None achieves a stable near-task hold. The results indicate that reach-and-hold is an unstable-dynamics stabilization problem, motivating learned selective impedance and predictive/internal-model control."
