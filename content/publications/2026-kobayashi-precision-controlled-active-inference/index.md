abstract: "This study tests a minimal continuous-time active inference model of quiet standing in which sensory reweighting is implemented through channel-specific precision control over vestibular, proprioceptive, and visual prediction errors. A one-link inverted pendulum estimates posture using a generalized-coordinate variational free-energy objective and selects ankle torque by minimizing the same objective under a restorative sensory goal. Selectively reducing the precision of unreliable visual or proprioceptive channels reduced perturbation-driven postural shifts by approximately 82%, whereas global fixed-precision reduction did not produce sensory reweighting. The results support a compact account of postural sensory reweighting as relative, context-selective precision control in a continuous active inference loop."
